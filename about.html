Snowboarding

My first time on a snowboard was at Shawnee ski resort in Pennsylvania, at age 9. From the beginning I was hooked. I spent as much time on the mountain as I could. In high school I joined Ski Club, and continued all the way to graduation. By that point, I was a good snowboarder and was extremely well versed in the activity. I went on to persue a career at Ski Barn and Big Snow in North New Jersey. After five years of hard work and extensive education in all things ski and snowboard, I decided to move to Colorado in October 2021. From there my skills and knowlege in snowboarding exploded. I learned how to ride the most extreme terrain, and build my skills and abilities off piste, and in the back country. I would spend many of my afternoons hiking in the backcountry around Loveland Ski Resort and the Continental Divide, averaging around 100 days a season. After 3 years and over 20 different resorts over the Western United States, I feel confident riding any terrain and difficulty, having done a helicoper drop just prior to moving back to the tristate. Snowboarding is a large part of my life and I look forward to many amazing days on the hill!
